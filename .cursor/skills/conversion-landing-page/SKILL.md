# Conversion landing (luxury service businesses)

## When to use

- **Homepage** or **campaign landing** where the goal is **inquiry / booking / WhatsApp**, not e-commerce cart frenzy.
- Salons, clinics, studios, consultancies — **trust + desire + clear next step**.

## Page architecture (recommended flow)

1. **Hero**: outcome + who it’s for + **one primary CTA** (Book / Consult / WhatsApp). Secondary link = lighter (View work / Services).
2. **Trust strip** (optional): awards, press, years, specialists — **minimal**, not logo soup.
3. **Services / outcomes**: scannable cards — **benefit-tinged** titles, not internal jargon only.
4. **Differentiator**: why us, process, or experience — calm copy, no hype adjectives wall.
5. **Visual proof**: gallery or case strip — **large**, not thumbnails only.
6. **Social proof**: 3 testimonials max above fold scroll depth — specific quotes, names, context.
7. **Objection handling** (light): FAQ or “what to expect” — short.
8. **Final CTA band**: repeat primary action with **reassurance** (response time, booking style).
9. **Footer**: hours, map, contact — conversion doesn’t end at button; people scan footers.

## Guide users to booking (without sleaze)

- Repeat **one primary verb** site-wide (e.g. “Book”) — synonyms OK in body copy, not five competing button labels.
- **Sticky** header CTA on long pages.
- **WhatsApp** as secondary global action if that’s how the business closes — visible, not a neon bubble unless brand allows.

## Hierarchy = conversion

- **One job per viewport**: don’t stack three equally loud CTAs.
- **Headline sells the feeling**; subhead explains the who/what; CTA is the handoff.
- **Pricing**: if shown, **clarity over tricks** — “from” is fine; hide nothing that causes distrust later.

## Emotional brand vs ask

- **Luxury**: generous whitespace, photography, restraint.
- **Still sell**: every major section should answer “why should I care?” or “what do I do next?”

## Mobile

- Hero CTA **thumb zone**; forms short; click-to-call / WhatsApp obvious.

## Checklist before ship

- [ ] First screen answers **what + where + next step**  
- [ ] Primary CTA visible again mid-page and end  
- [ ] Booking path ≤ 2–3 clicks from home (ideally)  
- [ ] No competing loud banners (popups, fake urgency)  
- [ ] Trust and clarity beat cleverness  

## Anti-patterns

- Fake countdown timers for a luxury salon  
- Stock “happy team” clip art  
- Ten equal-weight CTAs  
- Walls of text before any visual or proof
